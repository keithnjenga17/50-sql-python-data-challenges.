##Project Overview
This project analyzes social media platform market share trends using SQL, SQLite, and Python to evaluate platform growth, monthly performance changes, and year-over-year market share movements.

The analysis focuses on identifying:
platform growth trends,
month-over-month performance changes,
year-over-year market share movement,
and comparative platform performance across time.

##Business Problem
Digital analysts and marketing teams need to understand how social media platforms gain or lose market share over time in order to:
track industry trends,
evaluate platform growth,
monitor competitive performance,
and support strategic marketing decisions.

This project uses historical social media usage data to uncover trend-based business insights.

##Analysis Performed
The project explored:
Facebook year-over-year percentage share changes,
Twitter and YouTube month-over-month growth trends,
YouTube percentage changes during the final months of 2015,
and annual growth comparisons across Reddit, LinkedIn, and VKontakte.

##Key Insights
Social media platform market share changed significantly over time.
Facebook showed measurable year-over-year growth trends during the analyzed periods.
Twitter and YouTube demonstrated noticeable month-over-month fluctuations.
Time-series analysis revealed competitive differences between major social media platforms.
Window functions enabled efficient trend analysis without complex self-joins.

##Analytical Thinking Demonstrated
Applied window functions to compare current and historical platform performance.
Used percentage change calculations to evaluate platform momentum.
Combined CTEs and conditional logic to simplify time-series analysis workflows.
Structured SQL queries to transform raw historical data into trend-based business insights.
