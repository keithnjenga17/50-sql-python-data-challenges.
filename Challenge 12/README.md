##Project Overview
Social media companies, advertisers, and digital marketing teams need to understand how different user groups engage across multiple platforms. Understanding user behavior helps businesses improve audience targeting, increase engagement, and optimize digital campaigns.

This project analyzes social media usage patterns using SQL and Python to identify:
Users with high social media activity
Relationships between demographics and platform usage
Multi-platform engagement behavior
Social media usage trends across age groups
Gender distribution among highly active users

The analysis demonstrates practical analytics workflows including behavioral segmentation, conditional aggregation, demographic analysis, and percentage-based business reporting.

##Business Problem
Organizations operating in digital marketing and social media analytics need answers to questions such as:
Which users are highly active across multiple platforms?
Which demographic groups spend the most time on social media?
Are certain professions or education levels associated with heavier social media usage?
Which age groups demonstrate the highest engagement frequency?
What gender patterns exist among highly engaged users?

Without structured behavioral analysis, businesses struggle to:
Build accurate customer personas
Improve ad targeting strategies
Optimize content delivery
Understand engagement behavior across audience segments
Identify high-value digital audiences

##Key Insights
Multi-Platform Usage is Common Among Active Users
A measurable percentage of users maintained more than three social media accounts, indicating:
Cross-platform engagement behavior
Increased digital activity
Higher exposure to online content and advertising
Education and Profession Influence Platform Usage
Users with:
Master’s degrees
Management professions
showed stronger tendencies toward owning multiple social media accounts.
This suggests higher digital connectivity among professionally active and educated users.
Younger Users Show Higher Daily Engagement

The analysis revealed that users aged:
20–29
30–39
demonstrated strong “everytime” social media usage behavior, highlighting high engagement frequency within younger demographics.
Gender Differences Exist Among Highly Active Users
Among users with:
More than three social media accounts
High daily social media duration
there were observable differences between male and female participation rates.
This insight can support audience-specific campaign targeting.
