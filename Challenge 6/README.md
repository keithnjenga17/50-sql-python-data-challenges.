##Project Overview
This project analyzes electronics sales transaction data using SQL, SQLite, and Python to uncover customer purchasing behavior, spending trends, and revenue patterns.

The analysis focuses on:
customer spending progression,
repeat purchase behavior,
purchase frequency,
and high-value customer identification.

##Business Problem
Retail businesses need to understand how customers behave over multiple purchases in order to:
improve retention,
identify loyal customers,
optimize revenue strategies,
and increase repeat purchases.

This project uses transactional sales data to evaluate customer purchasing patterns and long-term customer value.

##Key Insights
Many customers increased spending between their first and second purchases.
Third-purchase revenue provided measurable insight into customer retention behavior.
A small segment of customers generated disproportionately high cumulative revenue.
Frequent buyers represented a critical customer segment for long-term revenue growth.
Consecutive credit card purchases revealed strong repeat purchasing behavior among certain customers.

##Analytical Thinking Demonstrated
Applied window functions to track customer purchase sequences over time.
Used cumulative revenue calculations to identify high-value customers.
Segmented buyers into frequent and occasional categories using purchase intervals.
Combined ranking logic and date analysis to evaluate repeat purchasing behavior.

##Data Visualization
A donut chart was created using Matplotlib to compare frequent buyers versus occasional buyers.
The visualization highlighted customer purchasing frequency distribution and retention behavior.
