##Project Overview
This project analyzes second-hand car market data using SQL, SQLite, and Python to evaluate vehicle pricing, mileage trends, and brand performance.

The analysis focuses on identifying:
the most and least expensive vehicles,
pricing patterns across manufacturers,
mileage efficiency,
and high-value car models within the used car market.

##Business Problem
Used car dealerships and automotive analysts need better visibility into:
pricing distribution,
premium vehicle segments,
mileage trends,
and brand-level performance.

The goal is to support data-driven pricing and inventory decisions using historical second-hand car data.

##Key Insights
Significant price variation exists across second-hand vehicle brands.
Premium manufacturers such as BMW, Audi, and Mercedes-Benz consistently dominated the high-price segment.
Window functions provided efficient ranking and comparative analysis across manufacturers.
Mileage trends revealed performance differences between vehicle models within the same brand.
Several models significantly exceeded average market pricing, indicating strong premium positioning.

##Data Visualization
A bar chart was created using Matplotlib to compare the most expensive car models across selected manufacturers.
The visualization highlighted pricing differences among premium vehicle brands.
